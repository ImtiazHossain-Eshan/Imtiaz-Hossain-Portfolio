## A different threat model

Deepfake research usually asks: is this image fake? Biometric privacy under
generative editing asks a different, subtler question: even after someone edits my
photo, can a face-recognition system still match it back to *me*?

Often, yes. Generative editors change pose, expression, or background while leaving
enough biometric signal intact that the edited output stays **re-linkable** to the
original person, someone who never consented to the edit. That is the gap Obscrowd
targets.

## Unlinkability, not un-editability

The framing shift at the core of this work: we do not try to prevent the edit.
Most prior methods do, and they tend to assume a single subject and one narrow
editing pipeline. Real photos are group shots with many faces and an open-ended
space of edits.

Instead, Obscrowd lets the image be edited and reduces the ability of
face-recognition systems to re-link the result to the real people, while keeping
the protected image **visually faithful** to the original. Every detected face in a
group image is protected jointly by a single imperceptible, face-localized
perturbation guided by soft masks.

## The pipeline

Protection is produced by a teacher-student-refinement pipeline:

- A **diffusion-guided teacher** generates strong, image-specific protection.
- A **lightweight student generator** distills the teacher for efficient
  inference-time deployment.
- An **inference-time refinement** stage sharpens the perturbation within a bounded
  perceptual budget.

Training combines teacher imitation with identity-disruption supervision from an
ensemble of face-recognition models and differentiable editing attacks, so the
perturbation is optimized to survive realistic edits rather than one fixed one.

## Does it work?

In the headline example, an instruct-pix2pix smile edit that leaves
face-recognition similarity at **0.957** on an unprotected image drops it to
**0.138** once protected, a large identity-disruption effect from a perturbation
that is visually imperceptible. The behavior holds across diverse editing and
processing conditions, and carries over to single-portrait settings too.

## The honest part

The paper is explicit that **reconstruction and compression remain open
challenges**. A determined adversary with strong purification or aggressive
re-compression can erode the protection. Naming that envelope is part of the
contribution: a privacy method that overclaims its guarantees is more dangerous
than one that states exactly where it stops working.
